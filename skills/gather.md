# Skill: Gather
## Populate a Document from Tacit Knowledge

---

## Purpose

Populate a target document by interviewing the operator — working through the document's structure section by section, capturing answers in-place, and treating the draft as WIP until the interview is complete.

---

## When to Use

- Any document that needs to be populated from the operator's tacit knowledge rather than from existing material
- The document has a defined structure (sections, phases, or dimensions) but its content is empty or incomplete
- Common targets: `resources/author.md`, a Brief, a spec, a strategy document, any structured artifact

---

## Procedure

1. **Read the target document and its governing context.** The governing context includes the Brief, the layer the document feeds into, the project memory, or whatever artifact defines the document's purpose. Do not work from the document in isolation — the right questions come from understanding what the document is for, not just what fields it has.

2. **Assess the document's nature before asking anything.**
   - **Structured document** (spec, form, template with defined fields): derive questions from the document's existing sections — work through them in order.
   - **Open document** (author input, a blank Brief, a strategy doc): derive questions from the document's purpose and governing context. Do not work from any pre-scaffolded sections — those impose structure before it has been earned. Let content surface first; structure emerges in the next layer.

3. **Work through the document accordingly.** For each section or theme, ask targeted questions and capture answers in-place. Do not present all questions at once — pace the interview one thread at a time.

4. **Treat everything as WIP.** Earlier captured content is not locked. If a later answer reveals an inconsistency with something already written, flag it immediately, revise the earlier content, and continue.

5. **Complete the interview.** The interview is done when the operator confirms — not just when all fields are filled. An operator may have more to add, or may want to stop early.

6. **Run a coherence pass.** Before declaring the document complete, check that all content is internally consistent, aligned with the document's stated purpose, and free of contradictions introduced during the interview.

7. **Declare complete.** Confirm with the operator that the document is ready to serve its intended role in the project.

---

## Notes on Question Quality

- Prefer specific over open-ended: *"What's the one thing people always misunderstand about X?"* over *"What do you want to say about X?"*
- Probe for examples and analogies — these are often the most useful raw material for a rationale or narrative
- If an answer is thin, follow up once before moving on
- If an answer implies a change to a higher-layer document (e.g., it contradicts the Brief), flag the upstream implication before capturing it

---

## Notes on WIP Handling

- The document is unstable during the interview — do not treat any captured section as final until the coherence pass is complete
- Revisions mid-interview are expected and healthy; they signal that the interview is surfacing real understanding, not just filling fields
- The coherence pass is not optional — it is what converts a set of interview answers into a coherent document
